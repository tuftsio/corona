Tufts CS Code for Good Summer Program

Last updated: May 7, 2020.

Overview

Due to COVID-19, many companies have suspended hiring and/or cancelled their summer internship programs, leaving students without valuable opportunities to improve their programming skills, gain real-world experience, envision future career paths, expand their networks, and contribute to meaningful projects. We would like to provide structure and support for students to pursue alternative projects to achieve these goals over the summer.

Projects

Students can decide whether they would like to work on a personal project or a project for a client. For those who are interested, we intend to match students, individually or in small teams, with nonprofits, small businesses, and other organizations in need of technical support. All students will work approximately 20 hours per week over an 8-week period on a project for their client, and will present their project to other students and interested members of the CS community at the end of the summer. We hope this will allow students to gain valuable technical skills in areas that interest them, while building community within the department and contributing to organizations in need!

We expect that projects will range in complexity: some will be reasonable for one student to work on, others may involve small teams of students working together, possibly with some students focused on project management and/or design.

Alumni Involvement

In order to provide the support and mentorship students will need to succeed, we are reaching out to the alumni community to engage with affected students in any way they can! Here are a few key ways we hope alumni will get involved -- but we are very open to other ideas and would love to hear from you! Please email Caroline Kaufman (class of 2020) with ideas/thoughts/questions/etc.

  • Project Coaches: Dedicated project coaches will work with a specific student or team of students to scope out their project and provide guidance on project management - timelines, goal setting, MVP features, etc. Expectations are:
  • Join initial call with client to understand project scope and expectations
  • Check in with student group at least twice per week to set goals and evaluate progress
  • Technical Experts: Do you have lots of experience with a particular language, framework, or technology? We’re looking for tech mentors to monitor dedicated Slack channels for their chosen technologies and help students find resources and resolve technical challenges. Examples: React, Django, Rails, Git, etc.
  • Professional Development: In addition to supporting ongoing technical projects, we would love for alumni to share their wisdom and experience with students! Possible ways to help: give a Zoom presentation on your career experiences or something you’re passionate about, review resumes at the end of the summer, conduct mock interviews at the end of the summer, introduce students to folks in your network who share their interests.

These are just a few examples of ways to get involved, but even if you’re not totally sure what you might be able to commit to, we hope interested alumni will join our Slack workspace and connect with students! If you are interested in participating in this program in any way, please fill out the interest form - there are lots of ways to get involved, and students would love to learn from you!
